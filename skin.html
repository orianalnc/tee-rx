Skin Conditions / Dermatology Solution

  • Acne Treatments
    Acne is the most common skin condition experienced by people around the world, but here at Tee Pharmacy we are here to help. Acne can range from mild to severe, and if not treated properly can lead to scarring. The severity of your acne will indicate which treatment is best for you.
    • Mild blemishes or non-inflammatory acne can usually be clear up with the help of non-prescription products containing Salicylic acid. This will help to unclog pores and prevent further acne.
    • For more serious acne combined with cysts or nodules you should see a dermatologist. Your specialist will prescribe a stronger treatment possibly containing sulfur, or colloidal.
    For mild acne our pharmacy specialists are here to walk you through our available products and find the treatment that is right for you. If you appear to have a more serious case of acne our staff can recommend you to a specialist.
  • Anti-fungal Treatments
    Fungal infections can manifest themselves in a number of ways, but most commonly through ringworm, athlete’s foot, fungal nail infections, vaginal thrush and severe dandruff. These conditions can usually be treated with anti-fungal treatments provided here at Tee Pharmacy. Work with our pharmacists to find the right treatment for you. Our selection includes:
    • Topical ointments: Applied directly to the affected area
    • Oral treatments: Taken in tablet or capsule form. This treatment method will be prescribed by your health care specialist.
    If your condition appears to be more serious your pharmacist may direct you to see your health care professional for hospital care.
  • Eczemas Treatments
    At Tee Pharmacy we provide a wide variety of treatments to help manage your condition. Eczemas are recognize by it symptoms such as itchy swollen rash, reddened Skin among others. Work with your health care specialist to find a combined treatment strategy and our staff will do all we can to provide the necessary tools. Our pharmacy is stocked with:
    • Over-the-counter treatments: such as moisturizers for breakouts
    • High moisturizing lotion and soaps: to help contain and prevent dry skin
    • Topical prescriptions: that contain steroids and PDE4 inhibitors to aid against rashes
    Learning to control eczema breakouts is a process, but our staff will work with you and your doctor to manage your condition and get you on your way.
  • Psoriasis Treatments
    Psoriasis is an immune-mediated skin condition that usually develops in people between the ages of 15 and 35. The disease causes raised, scaly, red patches to breakout on the skin. Since psoriasis is a skin condition, it requires diagnoses by a dermatologist. If you have been diagnosed with psoriasis your skincare specialist will help you come up with a plan of treatment. Here at Tee Pharmacy we know that psoriasis treatments are not one size fits all. There are several treatments that must be administered or performed by a dermatologist, while other treatments you can pick up from us.
    • Topical treatments: this is the first treatment method for psoriasis. Many of these products can be purchased over-the-counter, and contain salicylic acid and coal tar. Your dermatologist will inform you if you require a prescription medication.
    • Oral treatments: these treatments come in pill form and are used to treat the inflammation caused by psoriasis. This treatment method will be prescribed by your health care specialist.
  • Rosacea Treatments
    This skin condition mainly affects the face and while untreatable, can be controlled to a point. The first sign of rosacea is flushing, causing the skin to turn red. As the disease progresses it can cause symptoms such as burning, stinging sensations, permanent redness, skin spots and visible blood vessels. If you suspect you have rosacea you should speak to a dermatologist right away. Treatment for this condition usually requires a combination of solutions. Tee Pharmacy provides treatment options and our friendly specialists are here to help.
    • Creams and gels: that can be applied directly to the face are one of many over-the-counter treatment options.
    • Oral medications: come in pills and tablets, and will be prescribed by your dermatologist.
